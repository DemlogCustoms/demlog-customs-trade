# Demlog SEO audit — July 14, 2026

## Executive finding

The website is technically crawlable, but `demlogct.com` has only just moved from Shopify to Netlify. Google must recrawl the domain, replace its understanding of the former site and evaluate a new company against long-established customs brokers. An immediate first-page result for “customs broker Canada” is therefore not a realistic launch-day benchmark.

Estimated organic-search readiness after the corrections in this package: **74/100**.

- Crawlability and indexation controls: 17/20
- Technical delivery and performance readiness: 18/20
- On-page relevance: 16/20
- Content depth and differentiation: 11/20
- Authority, citations and backlinks: 4/20
- Trust and conversion readiness: 8/10

This is a readiness assessment, not a Google ranking guarantee.

## Corrections implemented

- Removed every public reference to the former $50 flat brokerage fee in English and French.
- Replaced pricing language with shipment-specific quotation language without changing the page layout.
- Strengthened the English homepage H1 to “Canadian Customs Broker for Commercial Imports.”
- Strengthened the French homepage H1 and both homepage descriptions.
- Changed the services H1 to “Canadian Customs Brokerage Services.”
- Added unique meta descriptions to the three published customs guides.
- Pre-rendered all 76 bilingual routes as static HTML instead of generating every page on demand.
- Added bilingual alternates to the XML sitemap.
- Updated sitemap modification dates for the changed pages.
- Corrected unknown URLs so they return a real 404 instead of a crawlable soft-404 page.
- Ensured the rendered document language switches between `en-CA` and `fr-CA`.
- Preserved canonicals, bilingual URLs, forms, PARS links, navigation and the approved visual system.

## What is already strong

- HTTPS works on the apex domain, with `www` redirecting to the preferred domain.
- The root URL redirects permanently to `/en`.
- Pages are indexable and the robots file points to the XML sitemap.
- English and French pages have self-referencing canonicals and hreflang links.
- The homepage has one H1 and a clear Canadian customs-broker focus.
- Dedicated pages exist for highway, air, ocean, courier, PARS, CARM, tariff classification, regulated goods, first-time importers and non-resident importers.
- Organization, Service and FAQ structured data are present where visible content supports them.
- CBSA licence verification, business contact details and an actual Canadian mailing address provide useful trust signals.

## Main remaining ranking constraints

### 1. The domain and replacement site are new to Google

Changing DNS does not instantly transfer rankings to new content. Google must crawl the Netlify version, process its canonicals and sitemap, and reassess each URL. Manual searches during this period are not a reliable performance report.

### 2. Competitor authority

Established firms such as Livingston and Farrow have older domains, extensive industry mentions, backlinks, branded searches and large content libraries. Demlog cannot reproduce those trust signals through metadata alone.

### 3. Service-page depth

The service pages target useful commercial-intent searches, but many currently share the same short explanatory structure. They need original, service-specific information before they can compete consistently. Avoid generating dozens of thin location or commodity pages.

### 4. Editorial depth

Only three short guides are published. The fastest defensible content opportunity is a focused Canadian importer knowledge base based on real customs expertise, official sources and practical examples.

## Ninety-day organic plan

### First 30 days

- Confirm indexing and fix any Search Console exclusions.
- Obtain accurate company listings and citations from relevant Canadian business and customs/logistics organizations.
- Ask legitimate clients, partners and professional organizations to link to Demlog where editorially appropriate.
- Expand the Canadian customs-clearance, highway/PARS and first-time-importer pages with original process details and FAQs.

### Days 31–60

Publish two substantial, sourced guides per month:

- How to appoint a customs broker in CARM
- First-time commercial importer checklist for Canada
- PARS release process and common border delays
- Documents a Canadian customs broker needs before arrival

### Days 61–90

- Expand CARM/RPP, regulated goods and non-resident importer content.
- Add real anonymized scenarios only when they reflect completed work and do not disclose client information.
- Review Search Console queries and improve pages receiving impressions but low click-through rates.

## Measurement

Track progress in Search Console, not by repeatedly typing one keyword into Google. The useful indicators are:

- Indexed pages
- Non-branded impressions
- Average position by query and page
- Click-through rate
- Qualified form submissions and calls
- Referring domains and legitimate industry citations

Reasonable early signs can appear within several weeks, but competitive national rankings commonly require sustained content, authority and engagement over months. No ethical SEO provider can guarantee a specific position.
